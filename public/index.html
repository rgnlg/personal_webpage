Olga Iarygina

Researcher in Human-Computer Interaction

I am a Research Assistant at the Human-Centred Computing research group at the University of Copenhagen, Denmark. My research is supervised by Professor Kasper Hornbæk.

Currently, I do research on the experience of meaning with technologies. With empirical investigations, I am trying to understand which interactions with technologies people consider meaningful and how important meaning is as a quality of interaction. The second area of my research is the theoretical state of HCI, where I try to understand how HCI uses, borrows, tests and develops theories.

In September, I will start my Ph.D. with cross-affiliation with Pioneer Centre of AI and Human-Centred Data Science Group at IT University of Copenhagen. My Ph.D. will be supervised by Assistant Professor Aske Mottelson and Professor Kasper Hornbæk. I am passionate about metascience, and my work will be focused on Replication Crysis in Extended Reality research. I am eager to explore the extent of the replication crisis in XR and establish the applicability of initiatives that have improved scientific practice in other research areas for XR research. I hope that the project will lead an international open science initiative to make both previous and future HCI research more robust.
